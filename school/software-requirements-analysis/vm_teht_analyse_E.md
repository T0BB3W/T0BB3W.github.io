# Assignment 3: Analysing Requirements

## Contents

1.  Quality criteria for Requirements

2.  Clarifying Requirements

3.  Conflict Resolution

4.  Prioritising Requirements

## Objectives

1.  Students are able to use quality criteria to refine their requirements

2.  Students are able to clarify requirements

3.  Students are able to identify and tackle conflicting requirements

4.  Students are able to assist in prioritising requirements

5.  Students are able to apply epic and snow card formats in requirements documentation

## Materials 

In Moodle

## Assignments

1)  **Check each of your requirement with a set of requirements quality criteria**. You may choose which criteria to use; they may be a subset of those presented in the materials, but should include at least 5 criteria).\
    **Refine your requirements as needed**.

2)  **Compile an Interaction Matrix** based on your refined requirements. Check for potential conflicting or overlapping requirements. Refine and clarify your requirements further to get rid of conflicts. You may need to compile a glossary, too.

3)  **Use the snow card format (table type**) to present your requirements.\
    You may omit some parts of the snow card - but do so with adequate consideration.

4)  **Prioritise** your requirements. You may use the more traditional approach – or the Kano approach – or combine the two. Add priorities to your requirements. (Add a note on how numbers should be interpreted: e.g. does 1 mean top or bottom priority.)\
    **Order** your requirements so, that the most important ones are first and least important ones last. Keep business requirements in mind while prioritising.

During the course, the requirement documents produced by participants may be utilised by the lecturer. The lecturer may compile a summary from the submitted documents (without authors' names) in their original form, edited, or merged with other documents.
