**Case: Sauna Slots**

*The “system”*

- A housing company named KallionKolot has 5 apartment buildings.

- There are 162 apartments in the KallionKolot housing company.

- KallionKolot has 3 saunas.

- House A has a sauna called A1. It is small and has 2 changing rooms.

- House B has 2 saunas B1 and B2. B2 is the largest, it rooms 12 persons at a time. It also has a club room with a fridge and a microwave oven. Each sauna in house B has 1 changing room.

- The sauna in house A is heated on Tuesdays at 17-18 for women and on Wednesdays at 17-18 for men as an after jogging relaxation.

- The caretaker of the housing company heats the saunas.

- The residents of KallionKolot may book a sauna slot to any the saunas from the caretaker, who keeps records of the booked sauna slots. The reservations are displayed on the door of each sauna and the caretaker is responsible to keep them up to date.

- A resident may book a single sauna slot or a regular (weekly) sauna slot, if there are free slots. The reservation should be made at least 1 week in advance.

- The duration of each sauna slot is 1 hour.

- Regular sauna slots are permanent until a resident cancels it.

- Sauna slots are paid based on a separate invoice.

- The cost of the sauna slot is set on the annual general meeting of the KallionKolot housing company. Regular sauna slots and single sauna slots have differing costs.

- During summer there are no regular sauna slots and only sauna B1 is heated.

- The user of the sauna is responsible for the tidiness of the sauna.

- The caretaker cleans the sauna regularly once a week and disinfects it once a month.

- The caretaker informs the residents of exceptional situations (for instance if a sauna is under reparation). These notices are displayed on the notice board of each house.

Problems

- Many sauna slots are not booked as the booking is somewhat demanding.

- Regular sauna slots are popular, but the most convenient time slots are usually taken.

- The resident must pay for the reserved slot even he would not use it. And electricity is spent uselessly.

- One does not always reach the caretaker when one wishes to book a single sauna slot.

- One does not always know in advance when the sauna urge wakes up.

- Some residents leave the sauna and the club room untidy.

- An unused sauna slot cannot be easily reassigned to another resident.

- Heating the sauna for only one slot is expensive.

Objectives

- To serve the residents better.

- Enable the residents to book a sauna slot anywhere and anytime.

- Spare on heating costs.

Note

- The KallionKolot housing company does not look for economical profit

- All apartments in the KallionKolot housing company have access to Internet.

- The residents are diverse, old people, students, families, forigners, …

- Turnover in KallionKolot housing company is small.

Constraints

- Budget is limited and sparse

- No major changes to sauna structures are not allowed.

- Sauna slots will be available only to residents.

- The solution must comply with legislations (e.g. GDPR).
