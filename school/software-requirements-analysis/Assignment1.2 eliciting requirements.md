Description of the domain / target area. (Tobias Weckström)

                         The ”system-as-is” is operating in an objectively narrow environment. It is
                         catering to the needs of the residents of 162 apartments in regard to
                         booking sauna slots from 3 separate saunas in a housing company with 5
                         apartment buildings. The system is quite lackluster and manual labour
                         heavy, since the caretaker of the housing company is in charge of heating
                         the saunas, cleaning and maintaining them regularly, and also managing
                         all of the bookings. The system is not designed for economical profit, and
                         the turnover in the housing company is small.

                         The ”system-to-be” will operate in exactly the same environment, but a lot
                         of changes can be made to make the system more profitable for the
                         housing company, even by a small margin, and lightening the workload for
                         the caretaker, resulting in a more productive worker, and more satisfied
                         customers (sauna users/residents). To begin, the heating of the sauna
                         should be automatized or semi-automatized, this would require some very
                         minor wiring changes and sensors installed, which would not be too
                         expensive. Costs could also be cut by the housing company by
                         outsourcing the cleaning and disinfecting of the saunas to a 3rd party,
                         enabling a slightly larger margin for profit.

                         Semi automized refers to a manual switch or interface from where the
                         saunas could be switched on/off and adjusted remotely by the caretaker,
                         providing information to when the sauna is ready.

                         A fully automized approach will be more expensive since the migration is
                         expected to be messy and requires an initial workload increase from the
                         caretaker and other stakeholders. The capital expenses are also higher
                         because an additional platform needs to be put in place. The fully
                         automized system will work in a way that, the residents can book/unbook
                         their sauna slots, regular or irregular, and the sauna will be automatically
                         heated/cooled down for even a small duration during a gap slot, saving
                         energy costs for the housing company. The invoicing will also benefit from
                         this approach, cutting on accounting costs. This can also open up the
                         sauna slots for a bigger audience, e.g. night-shift workers, spontaneous
                         sauna goers and so on, creating a slightly better profit margin for the
                         housing company, since the caretaker is not reachable around the clock
                         and on weekends. The system will allow users to give feedback, easing the
                         process of finding the culprits of untidiness etc. Book keeping will be more
                         effective since an extensive record of sauna goers, heating hours etc. can
                         be established, and used for diagnostics to better the system. This
                         approach would be very profitable in the long run due to the above stated
                         reasons and increasingly expensive energy costs.

Jorma’s birthday (ID: s_02)

Jorma’s birthday is coming up. Jorma lives in an apartment in the KallionKolot complex. Jorma is
going to have friends over for his birthday to celebrate a few weeks from now. Jorma has a regular
sauna slot in sauna B1 on Friday evenings for one hour, but he would like to book the B2 sauna with
the club room for 2 hours. He tries contacting the caretaker by phone with no success. He finds a
contact form for the caretaking service online and shoots a message in hopes of haggling himself a
two-hour window for sauna B2 instead of his regular B1 hour. The caretaker sends an email to Jorma
on his birthday week, but it goes straight to his junk mail. Fortunately, Jorma is tech-savvy and ends
up finding the email. The caretaker lends out the B1 hour to someone else, and invoices Jorma with a
slight reduction the following month for his 2 hours in B2 with club room.



Teppo’s jog (ID: s_03)

Teppo is just finishing a long jog on a rainy Wednesday evening. He tries breaking his own personal
record and is struggling but is comforted by knowing he will soon get to the warm sauna. Teppo is
home at 17 sharp and puts his clothes to dry, puts on a bathrobe, and grabs his stuff headed to the
sauna. Teppo is devastated when he arrives at the sauna as he sees a note on the door. He
approaches the note to find out that today the sauna is under maintenance. He is in disbelief, how
could this not have been announced beforehand, he is a paying customer after all. He calls the
caretaker and is told an email has been sent out, must have ended up in the junk mail, he figures.
Instead of lecturing the caretaker on his digital skills, Teppo explains the situation and asks for
another sauna spot or financial recompensating. Money is tight for the housing company, so the
caretaker offers Teppo the B1 spot on Friday. Teppo agrees and it’s settled.
