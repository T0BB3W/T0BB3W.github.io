# Assignment 2: Eliciting Requirements

## Sisältö

1.  Familiarizing with the domain area

2.  Stakeholders

3.  Other sources of requirements

4.  Methods for requirements elicitation

## Learning objectives

1.  The student can describe the domain area.

2.  The student can identify sources of requirements.

3.  The student can identify stakeholders.

4.  The student can describe methods for requirements elicitation.

5.  The student can describe preliminary requirements as a User Story -type table.

## Materials

In Moodle

## Assignments

1.  Sauna-CASE, part 2:

> Familiarize yourself with the materials, the Sauna CASE, your personalised user stories / narratives, and additional user stories from this week’s Moodle to **create an informal (or formal) description of the domain / target area**.\
> The description should be shortish, but it should provide a solid overview of the operations within the target area. **Identify and present the stakeholders** related to the target area. If You wish, You can illustrate the operations using a swim lane activity diagram(s). (Available in MsVisio.)

2.  Sauna-CASE, part 3:

> Select 4-8 stories from the narratives produced in the group in Task 1 (last week).\
> These stories should, in your opinion, comprehensively depict the stakeholders, objectives, and operations related to the Case Sauna slots. Choose stories that **provide substantial value for the improvement of the operations**. Ensure that at least 2 stories are chosen per group member.
>
> If the previously made stories do not suffice, supplement your collection by creating a few new stories. Assign an **individual identifier** to all the stories for easy referencing in the future (traceability).
>
> Based on the chosen stories, identify preliminary requirements for the software. Recognize both functional and non-functional requirements, as well as potential constraints. Start to consider which objectives are essential for implementing the sauna slots software and which are optional but beneficial. **Identify approximately 10-20 requirements**.
>
> Gather the identified preliminary requirements into a table following the **user story / epic format (who-what-why)**. Include a brief unique identifier for each requirement, indicate whether it's functional, non-functional, or a constraint, specify its necessity (essential or optional/beneficial), and provide the identifier of the personalised story it's related to.
>
> Finally, **submit your epics** (table of requirements) as a text document.\
> Include the new personalised stories, if such were produced.

During the course, the requirement lists produced by participants may be utilised by the lecturer.\
The lecturer may compile a summary from the submitted requirement lists (without authors' names) in their original form, edited, or merged with other requirements.
