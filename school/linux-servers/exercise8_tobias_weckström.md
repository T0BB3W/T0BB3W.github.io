Permissions

            Permissions mean who can do what with: normal files(a), directories(d), symbolic
            links(l), special files (b & c). The aforementioned file types and their respective
            characters represent the first character in the string of 10 characters which describes the
            permissions of said files, also as seen in the screenshot. For example -rw-rw-r-- , would
            mean that the owner of the file (first three characters after the filetype) can read and
            write r & w. The following three characters are for the group permissions, also r & w. The
            last three characters are for all others, in this case only r as in read. The third one is x for
            execute, and these permissions can be numerised as follows : r=4 w=2 x=1. In the
            screenshot you can see my usage of the chmod change modifications command and
            using the numerical approach instead of the longer string method.
