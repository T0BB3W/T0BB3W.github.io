# Reflection on Project Meetings

We had only one project meeting at school which all three members were able to attend, in the first meeting we were basically just sparring each other with ideas, we came up with 3 different project ideas at first, but we quickly decided to go with the laundry service project, since everyone in our group could envision it being an enticing subject to work on. The idea was mine and I have been thinking about a business like this for a long time, so the decision making and creative thought process felt very intuitive.

Aside from this instance, we had one virtual meeting that took place in April. That meeting lasted several hours, in which we outlined actionable goals for the project plan, started assigning roles and working on preliminary versions on several of the chapters. At this stage everyone was still very confused of how a project plan like this should be done, scouring through the provided framework and feeling lost. I assured my group, or rather, at this point I already would call us a team, since our team co-operation and cohesion could be felt in the meeting, that the most important steps for now would be to start producing content. In several of the chapters it felt like the content we were producing was overlapping, so several revisions and versions of said project plan would be required.

Furthermore, we had a meeting in person at my office where we did deep work on the project in unison for around 3 hours. In that dusky cellar office, it truly felt like we were an actual team, these guys, Anh and Tiwari, really felt like a perfect match for teamworking. Everyone seemed to enjoy the atmosphere and the crazy spurt of productivity that occurred, I could not have been happier personally with the team cohesion and work effort demonstrated by our group.

# Evaluation of My Project Management skills

My experiences regarding the project meetings were as described earlier very pleasant, the lads gave me an incredibly good place to really apply myself in my best regard. To me it seemed like Anh and Tiwari have their own strengths, but not necessarily the creativity or the required fierceness that is required when making executive decisions regarding broad projects like this, where you really don’t know where you should start, not having done this sort of project plan earlier.

To our team’s advantage I have a lot of experience acting as a group/project leader from my earlier career, and military. I also noticed that Anh and Tiwari really enjoyed the freedom I gave them regarding the project but still gave them the required guidelines if there was any retardation in our workflows, I also double checked with them of course to not seem like being too pushy or dictatory.

Communication was very easy, we communicated mainly through Microsoft Teams, where I usually was the one to start a new post, to get an overlook of where we are currently, and what are the next steps required to complete this assignment in due time.

Time-management is my curse and the bane of my existence. There is something deeply wrong in my chain of thought regarding timelines and deadlines. I am almost without exception the student that submits his assignments late and leaves work to the last moments. I really can’t help it, but certain productivity measures and for example trying to cut out instant gratifications from my day-to-day activities have helped massively. I am also getting medical assistance from professionals currently regarding these issues. It is a constant battle, but I learn to cope with it better each passing day.

# Learning and development

Working on this project has provided me with a lot of insight regarding the practical aspects of project management. Prior to this my knowledge and understanding were mostly in completely other sorts of projects. I gained a lot of experience in coordinating a team towards a common goal.

One of the key skills I developed is balancing leadership with collaboration. I found myself naturally stepping into a leader’s role while still ensuring everyone had autonomy and decisiveness. This created a productive environment where Anh and Tiwari could contribute their A-game while I maintained the project’s direction.

# Conclusion

This project has fundamentally shifted my understanding of effective project management. What stands out most is that successful management isn't just about technical knowledge or rigid adherence to frameworks, but rather about creating synergy between team members and adapting leadership styles to the situation at hand. My experience with Anh and Tiwari has shown me that when team chemistry works, productivity soars far beyond what could be achieved through mere technical competence.

The practical implications for my future career are significant. Having experienced firsthand how effective leadership can transform a potentially confusing assignment into a cohesive project, I feel even better equipped to take on managerial roles in professional settings then I did before.
