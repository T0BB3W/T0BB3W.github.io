Sales Role Play Self Review

            1. Start 0,75/1 points

                Firstly, building rapport went well in my opinion, there was not much to discuss since
                the role play was kind of in a restrictive environment. I asked how the customer’s
                (Thanh Ha Nguyen) week has been so far, and he assured me it has been pleasant,
                he had gotten some time with his children. I let the customer know that I was joyed
                of hearing these news aswell to establish rapport. I tried making sure before the role
                play that Thanh was on the same page, since I was representing NVIDIA and
                especially B2B sales with a emphasis on data centres and AI Processing, that he
                could play along best to his ability. He somehow misunderstood it completely though
                and derailed quite hard in the start, talking about personal computing requirements. I
                got the conversation back on track after a brief confusion.

            2. Needs assessment 3/3 points

                I had prepared quite an extensive manual for this sales role play, it was actually too
                extensive in retrospect. I could have gotten rid of a lot of information and perhaps
                grouped or color coded the contents of my manual for quick reference, I found
                myself scrambling through the document to find relevant information in an
                unprofessional manner.

                This particular manual however gave me a great benefit in the needs assessment. I
                was able to quickly present several technical questions regarding the customers
                (Apple was his respective company in this role play) needs. I mentioned Apple’s co-
                operation with OpenAI in their apple intelligence applications, some points about
                how it would be better to handle these sort of operations in house on your own
                hardware, instead of relying on another company for these services, being able to
                steer the conversation to a satisfying point for me to start providing the customer
                with solutions, and actually highlight their need for our services.

            3. Presenting solution / recommendations 2/2 points

                I was able to present solutions on two different price points, since the customer was
                very cost-attentive. I provided excellent arguments for why our products are the
                superior in the market. I told the customer a lot about future proofing, and the
                immediate benefits aswell as future benefits of our product, even being able to give
                some warranty promises according to official Nvidia documentation.

            4. Handling customer questions and objections 1/1 points

                My reviewers let me know that I handled the objections very well, and I agree. I went
                to great lengths assuring the customer that any difficult questions he might ask, will
                be addressed by one of my “technical architects”, and that our respective teams
                (since it was a b2b situation) could iron out some of the more technical details, which
                I could not answer.
