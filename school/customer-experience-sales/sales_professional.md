# Sales professionalism from the perspective of an Information Technology Student

Defining an all around good sales person can be difficult in the modern times, given the exponentially increasing amount and complexity of determining factors, such as: sales channels, sales platforms, environmental factors, and so on. From my own experience working in sales, I have seen many different personalities work in very different ways to achieve excellent results in their sales process, that no one true definition can be given.

In academia, a good salesperson can be defined by a mix of interpersonal skills, ethical integrity, emotional intelligence, and extensive product knowledge, as according to Spiller (2022, 53-66).

Sales is also moving from a purely capitalistic spectacle of quantities sold, to a more people-oriented industry. This is achieved with modern business intelligence tools and enormous amounts of data, which also leads to sales professionals who can harness these tools being able to get a substantial edge on their peers. Instead of purely relying on sales of a novel or competing product in it’s respective market, companies are moving towards creating long-lasting customer relationships, leading to returning customers. According to Spiller (2022, 9), the golden rule in selling today is: Don’t make a sale, create a customer! Subscription based payment models are also more popular than ever before, which puts emphasis on continuous customer satisfaction.

The general trend in the sales professionals video interviews I watched, were also very much in support of my earlier statements. All 3 of the sales professionals talked about genuinely trying to solve customer problems through being a great listener, relationship building salesperson, and not aggressively pushing products and numbers. However interviewee 3, Kim Qvick, former B2B sales professional, doctoral researcher, mentioned that at the end of the day treating sales like mathematics is beneficial, more contacts and leads, more closed deals. (Müürisepp September 2025)

Interviewee 1 wanted to stay anonymous, and is a Regional Sales Manager covering Italy, Spain, and Portugal, selling fire-extinguishing systems. (Hirvonen Prieto September 2025)

Lastly, interviewee 2, Ms. Nguyen Hong Anh, Head of Industrial and Commercial leasing at a real estate firm in Vietnam, was very thural and extensive in her statements. (Mẫn September 2025)

Key success factors stated by the 3 professionals, were relatively soft skills, such as: relationship building, face-to-face interaction and enjoying the interactive and multifaceted nature of the job. This definitely proves their consensus on that sales roles truly can be for anyone who is up for the challenge. Interviewee 2 even emphasized several times that you definitely wil not be stuck in a back office to work if you decide to get into sales.

Reflecting on what I have learned about sales during this course, and in my own experience working in different sales environments, as a sales professional I have very much shifted to this customer-centric approach. I used to work in a multimarket sales team briefly for around 4 months in 2023 before I started my studies, and after that I work as a sole practicioner doing different jobs in the restaurant, construction, and hospitality business. My character supports building strong, long-lasting relationships, and most my clients are aquired through extensive networking and word of mouth. I have even done some quite extensive work professionally for close friends & family, proving that the mantra “don’t mix friends and business” is becoming increasingly outdated.
