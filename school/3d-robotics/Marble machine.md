# Marble machine


## Slide 1: The machine that does nothing, but in a spectacular way

3D + Robotics coursework suggestion



## Slide 2: 1. Lifts and Elevators

Spiral Lifts: A rotating spiral that lifts marbles upwards to higher levels of the machine.
Bucket Elevators: Buckets attached to a chain or belt lift marbles to the top and drop them onto a track.
Staircase Elevator: A step-by-step lifting mechanism where marbles are pushed upwards by moving steps or levers.
iltasanomat



## Slide 3: 2. Tracks and Ramps

Curved Tracks: Tracks with gentle curves and slopes that allow marbles to smoothly roll down.
Loop-the-Loops: Create loops where marbles gain enough speed to complete a full circle.
Zigzag Ramps: A zigzag pattern where marbles move back and forth as they descend.
Figure-8 Track: A track in the shape of an 8 that adds an extra layer of visual interest.
Etsy.com



## Slide 4: 3. Tunnels and Bridges

Clear Tunnels: Transparent tubes or tunnels where marbles are visible as they travel through.
Swinging Bridges: A bridge that tilts back and forth, causing marbles to sway before moving on.
cgtrader



## Slide 5: 4. Interactive Elements

Plinko Board: A vertical board with pegs where marbles bounce and randomly choose a path as they descend.
Paddle Wheels: Rotating paddles that catch marbles and fling them onto different tracks.
Seesaws: Marbles roll onto a balanced seesaw, tipping it and rolling onto different paths.
Pinball-Style Bumpers: Spring-loaded bumpers that change the direction of the marbles when they hit.
Plinko-boards.com



## Slide 6: 5. Sorting Mechanisms

Color Sorters: A system that uses sensors to detect marble colors and directs them to different paths.
Size Sorters: Funnels or gates that sort marbles based on size.
Magnetic Sorting: Use magnets to sort magnetic marbles (like steel ones) from non-magnetic ones.
Instructables.com



## Slide 7: 6. Speed Control Elements

Funnels and Spirals: Marbles enter a funnel and spiral downwards, gradually slowing down as they reach the exit.
Brake Tracks: Slightly inclined tracks with textured surfaces to slow down marbles.
Corkscrews: Vertical corkscrew ramps where marbles spiral downwards, slowing down the descent.
3dptk.com



## Slide 8: 7. Noisemakers and Sound Elements

Xylophone Bars: Tracks where marbles roll over xylophone bars, producing musical notes.
Bells: Small bells that marbles strike as they pass.
Drums: Marbles drop onto small drums or taut membranes to create rhythmic sounds.



## Slide 9: 8. Loops and Recirculations

Continuous Loops: Design sections of the track where marbles can loop around and return to the start.
Switchbacks: Sections where marbles reverse direction, moving back through previously traveled areas.



## Slide 10: 9. Interactive Displays

Scoreboards: A simple counter that tallies how many marbles have passed a certain point.
Light Sensors: LED lights that activate when a marble passes a sensor.
LED Light Paths: Light up a path with LEDs as a marble passes, creating a glowing trail.



## Slide 11: 10. Gravity-Powered Devices

Gravity Wells: Marbles spiral into a funnel-like structure before dropping into a hole.
Rolling Hills: Small hills that marbles roll over, picking up and losing speed.



## Slide 12: 11. Marble Launchers

Catapults: A mechanism that flings marbles onto the next section of the track.
Slingshots: Marbles are pulled back and released to shoot down a track.



## Slide 13: 12. Obstacle Courses

Maze: A section where marbles have to find their way through a complex maze.
Rotating Barriers: Sections where rotating barriers periodically block or allow marbles to pass.



## Slide 14: 13. Endless Conveyor Systems

Helical Conveyor: A helical track that keeps lifting marbles in a spiral upward while feeding them into another track.
Chain Conveyors: A chain-driven conveyor that continually lifts and drops marbles.
