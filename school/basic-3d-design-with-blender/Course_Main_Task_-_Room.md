Heikki HietalaIT Dept. Malmi Campus HAAGA-HELIA University of Applied Sciences



## Slide 2: Your task is to make a room

In the room, you should have
A table
A couple of chairs (you can decide what kind of chairs)
Smaller objects like lamps and decorative items
You can use all meshes you have made during the course, I will not mind if the objects are unlikely to be found in a room (logic is turned off)
The total number of objects is at least 8 to 12
You also need lighting and camera
Walls need to be treated with wallpaper or paint with fiberglass texture
Floor needs some material and texture too



## Slide 3: Sample room



## Slide 4: This room is a cube

With one vertex removed it gives you a nice space to play with
Separate the walls and floor to make materials easier to assign (select them one by one and use P / by Material)
Place a few lamps in it (I would like to see a desk lamp like that with a spot inside it)
Render a few images from different angles
Please also do a couple of close-ups of your objects



## Slide 5: A second option for this task

If you wish, you can build just a desk
The desk then needs to have 8 to 12 objects just as the room
The desk needs to be complete with drawers
A desk lamp is needed, but you can decide on all other objects yourself
The same render requirements as with the room
Example of a desk. You need not copy this but I would like to see one.Source: Worrall’s Antiques http://www.burrellsantiquedesks.com/images/antique-desks/332-antique-desk/antique-partners-desk-3.jpg
