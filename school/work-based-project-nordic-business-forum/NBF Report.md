# Nordic Business Forum - Report

## What were your tasks at the event?

At the event, my designated function was First Class Host. I was tasked with a group of 25 individuals from S-Group who were all First Class ticket holders, this was a little heavier workload than other First Class hosts, since they were designated much smaller groups (7-8 individuals), but I had a lot of help from our group support member and core team member Janne. My tasks at the event were: Welcoming guests to the event, which included checking them in, and providing the guests with their badges, helping them check in their belongings at the cloakroom, and escorting them to the first class lounge, and any other place at the event that was needed. In the weeks/days leading up to the event I also contacted every single guest and arranged their taxi’s to and from the event, this took quite some time given the sheer amount of guests assigned to me.

## What observations did you do: what worked well, what could have been improved?

In my opinion all the core tasks I was assigned were quite effortless, but during the busy hours in the morning, I needed some assistance from other group members, since I had so many guests to care for. Not many improvements come to mind, I was very impressed by the level of all the preparations and training for the event.

## Grade your teamwork skills (1-5).  Give grounds to your opinion. 

I would grade my own teamwork skills a solid 5, however there is always room for small tweaks and improvement. As mentioned earlier, given my large group size, I was assigned very little shifts in my schedule, this lead to me being a sort of wild card, I was at all times ready to assist other team members with tasks, and did so often. For example if someone needed to take care of something, go to the bathroom, or wanted a small break, I would come in and manage their shift/tasks for the time being.

## What did you learn when working at the event?

That teamwork is extremely important at an event of this scale, you constantly need to communicate with other team members to ensure a good experience for the guests.

## What did you learn about the arrangements of this event?

That the groundwork for the event arrangements has to be started months/years in advance.

## What was your input in customer experience at the event?

Resolving issues quickly, being readily available to my guests at all times.

## How did your skills and competencies develop during this experience?

I developed many aspects of my competencies, but mostly I would say that my teamwork and communication skills were tested.

## Which skills would you particularly like to improve in the future, and why?

Organizing skills, however with the contacting of the clients I already organized all my available information quite well into tables etc, in my private notes, so I could readily find all relevant information.

## What challenges did you face while working at the event? How did you solve them?

Some guests needed a place to hold a work meeting during the day for one hour, and the Framery booths were only available for 30mins at a time, but I managed to get a 1hr reservation for my guests via Janne.

## How did facing these challenges impact your work and learning?

I learned that everything is possible through the right channels!

## How did your entrepreneurial attitude show at the event?

I acted very professionally, even to such an extent that it would benefit me, if I were to work with these individuals again, I got a lot of good personal feedback from my guests. I also acted on all problems without hesitation and quickly, keeping everyone involved up to date with status updates.

## How did this experience influence your perception of your professional skills?

asd

## Was there a situation where you realized you needed more knowledge or skills? How could you develop them in the future?

Not really, the training for the event was very well done, and all questions I had were answered quickly and precisely.

## How did you experience interaction with others?

With other team members, very well, the teamwork and group cohesion was definitely to our advantage. Interaction with a few guests was challenging, since they didn’t seemingly want to be contacted, not returning calls or texts.

## Did you have to take responsibility or lead situations? If yes, how did you manage, and what did you learn?

Nothing particular comes to mind, but I would say at maybe a few occasions I had to take responsibility or lead situations, and I managed very well. I learned that no challenge is too big, and if you don’t know something, find out!
