# Concerns about data security

I am personally very concerned about data security in this growing age of data collecting, and the selling of data for profit. It was quite a few years ago when I started noticing, that even whilst my microphone permissions and cookies were turned off on my smartphone, the advertisements were getting more and more targeted towards my recent trends.

I have been aware of data collection since a long time ago, as having used the internet and computers since under 10 years of age, but in no way had it become so evident and in your face as it is today. Nowadays I practice extreme caution when browsing the web and using apps and programs.

## Article: Google Chrome pushes ahead with targeted ads based on your browser history

Google has been rolling out a new feature called “Enhanced Ad Privacy” which does quite the opposite. It is a technology which, unless turned off, allows websites to target the user with ads tailored to their online activities based on their browser histories.

Google are dropping support for third-party cookies next year. And will be looking to cash in on it themselves. The mega-corp has developed a variety of replacement technologies, such as the Topics API that will allow ad targeting to continue without cookie-based tracking, claiming no privacy consequences.

It basically works like this: rather than using cookies to track people around the web and figure out their interests from the sites they visit and the apps they use, websites can ask Chrome directly, via its Topics JavaScript API, what sort of things the user is interezsted in, and then display ads based on that. Chrome picks these topics of interst from studying the user’s browser history.

Depending on what Chrome version you’re using, and whether you’ve been selected to start using the new feature, you can switch this functionality off and on by visiting *chrome://settings/adPrivacy* and/or *chrome://settings/privacySandbox* in your chrome browser.

I chose this article because it is very relevant, only little over a week old as of today, and I don’t particularly like to support Google’s advertising profits. Increasingly sensoring search results and actively trying to kill data privacy does not sit well with me. Google products are very good and usable, but remember, if the product is free, and there is no ads, then you are most likely the data they are selling.
