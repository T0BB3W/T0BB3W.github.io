# The Innovative City® Program - Call for Research Initiatives 20XX   

The Innovative City® is a joint development program of the Bravo University and the City of Duckburg. The objective of the program is to improve the appeal of the Greater Duckburg area and sustainable urban development by utilizing R&D cooperation.  

The program seeks potential collaboration opportunities, coordinates ongoing research cooperation and develops practices that promote R&D operations, from initial research project ideas to the efficient utilization of the research results. Innovation is sought with multidisciplinary collaboration extending over borders in different sectors.  

A total of 27 collaborative projects coordinated by Bravo University have been launched during 2020-2022. The total funding for the project is about 12 million euros, with City of Duckburg, public organizations, companies and the EU as the major financing partners.  

We are now calling for new research ideas for four themes:  

- Urban senior citizens  

- Sustainable construction  

- Urban public transportation in the future  

- Attractive urban housing.  

## Call for Initiatives 

Successful research initiatives should aim at cooperation between Bravo University and the units of the City of Duckburg and search for new information, technologies and procedures that promote sustainable urban development.  

The call for research initiatives is primarily aimed at:  

- The management and personnel of public offices and utilities of the City of Duckburg ; and  

- Professors, research staff and students at the Bravo University. The participation of other parties having an interest in cooperation with Bravo University and the City of Duckburg in the call is also allowed.  

## Assessment 

The assessment of research initiatives is a two-step process, carried out by an assessment panel chosen for each project theme. In the first stage, an assessment of research initiatives is performed based on the application. Assessment criteria consist of:  

- Novelty of the initiative  

- Utilization potential of the results  

- Promotion of sustainable development  

- Multidisciplinary and multisectoral features.  

During the second step, the research initiatives with the greatest potential are invited to give an oral presentation, with an opportunity for the presenter to discuss the initiative with the assessment panel. The assessment panel will select the research initiatives they propose for further development, based on the presentations and discussion. The steering group for the Innovative City® Program will confirm the final selection. The panel and the steering group can suggest combining ideas that can complement each other. The assessment process is completely confidential.  

The assessment panel for each theme consists of four to six experts from the City of Duckburg units and other potential collaborative organizations and a project funding expert from the Bravo University.  

## Further Development  

Innovative City Program® will offer the three to five research initiatives selected for further assistance with the development of the project plan, searching for potential partners and funding opportunities and providing support for the funding applications of the projects. A preparatory workshop will be organized for the successful initiatives from the application process. The workshop aims at specifying a project plan and promoting the communication and commitment of potential partners.  

The objective is to find partial project financing (about 5 %) from the public offices and utilities of the City of Duckburg .The potential main sources of project funding (60-70 %) are the Tekes Technology Programs. Other potential financiers include companies, municipalities and organizations, ministries and the European Union.  

The projects that are launched will be included in the project activities of the Innovative City® Program, offering a synergic network of researchers and practical operators to support the implementation of the project, as well as tools to promote communication, networking and implementation of the results.
