Personal brand aspirations and digital footprint

Main points of the parts of the Introduction and the Findings and Discussion.

                           The main points that struck me from the Introduction part were that
                           everyone is individually branded nowadays, even though they might not be
                           self aware of it, and that there is a need for empirical research on personal
                           branding. Jacboson’s research seeks to fill that gap by focusing on the
                           personal branding of social media managers, since they provide critical
                           insight on the subject, it analyzes how people working in social media
                           brand their own identity and how personal branding is used to market
                           themselves to gain and maintain employment. “In this research, personal
                           branding is defined as the process of developing, harnessing and
                           classifying personal information and providing a comprehensive narrative
                           for others to easily understand one’s identity---often using social media.”
                           (Jacobson 2020, 715-716.)

                           In the Findings and Discussion parts a “future audience” is introduced in
                           relation to social media management and personal branding being a full-
                           time job or hustle, also described as an “always-on-the-job-market”. The
                           future audience is for example a future employer or followers. It will no
                           longer suffice to only cater to the present audience, the social media
                           profiles have to be carefully curated to appeal to the present audience and
                           future audience alike, to hinder stagnation in what is described as an
                           entrepreneurial field. Several of the 20 participants are interviewed and
                           quoted throughout the text, with valuable insight into the unstability of
                           social media management as a profession. (Jacobson 2020, 719-723.)
                           The internet is a massive archive of past and present information, be it
                           personal photographs, posts, opinions and more. What one might at some
                           point in time considered as being private or not of interest to say, future
                           employers or investors, that is not the case anymore.

Personal brand aspirations and digital footprint

                           I have somewhat of a personal brand, I have profiles on LinkedIn,
                           Facebook, X (aka. twitter) and Instagram. They are very rarely updated
                           and my X account is strictly personal, no followers or even any personal
                           details. I stopped using Facebook several years ago because of their
                           outright dystopian advertising strategies, but still have a profile. My
                           instagram is private, I use it for direct messaging my friends and posting
                           personal pictures maybe once every few months.Instagram’s ad policy is
                           absolutely horrendous aswell, and keeps getting worse after facebook
                           acquired them. LinkedIn is more of a digital and interactive CV. The
                           audiences vary a lot and are very curated, such as the content itself
                           (Jacobson, 2020).

                           acquaintances, friends and family. When the time comes, I will be crafting
