# What is the CIA, and why is it important

Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability, are the three core principles that form the foundation of IS programs and controls.

Confidentiality stands for the correct handling of sensitive information, it should only be accessible to authorized users, preventing data breaches and unauthorized access.

Integrity guarantees that data remains accurate, complete and unaltered, to maintain trust in information systems.

Availability means that data and systems are accessible when needed, preventing disruptions to information systems.

# Search and explain what are NIS2 Directive and ISO27001 standards, and why those might be important

The NIS2 Directive is a directive by the European commission that establishes 18 different critical sectors regarding information security across the EU, this came into force in January 2023. The directive mandates each member state of the EU to adopt a national cybersecurity strategy, to protect their network and information systems, their users, and other affected individuals from cyberattack incidents and threats.

ISO27001 is the world’s best-known standard for information security management systems (ISMS) and defines the requirements ISMS must meet, providing companies of any size and from all sectors with guidance for establishing, implementing, maintaining and continually improving an ISMS.

Both are very important for regulatory compliance, risk mitigation, and creating trust in information systems.
