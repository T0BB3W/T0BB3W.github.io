## Slide 2: IoT Devices

An IoT Device is typically made up of a circuit board with sensors attached that uses Wi-Fi to connect to the internet. For example:
Temperature and humidity sensors in an AC unit.
Motion / Inertial sensors in a robot vacuum.
Smart Speaker microphones…
The list is almost endless. Most electronic appliances these days, big or small does come with some sort of sensors for current or future application in IoT ecosystems.
This does however propose some challenges from the perspective of data privacy, given many end users are unaware of truly the amount of data collected by IoT devices. But fear not, the IoT ecosystem is an evolving field of study, connects vendors and companies, to make solution planning simple for efficient, reliable and secure IoT Systems.
IoT devices also use actuators. While sensors, sense; actuators act. They receive a signal or a command and, on its basis, they cause an action. As an example, actuators may control the heating and cooling in an AC unit.



## Slide 3: Connectivity

IoT is a network involving devices, sensors, cloud and actuators. These need to interconnect with one another to be able to decipher data and consequently perform an action. Connectivity forms the second piece of the puzzle in the complex world of the IoT ecosystem.
IoT devices typically send telemetry from their attached sensors to cloud services in a system. However, other types of communication are possible such as a cloud service sending commands to your devices. Some examples include:
A device monitoring a reactor sends an alert when the temperature exceeds a certain value.
A cloud service sets the temperature for a thermostat device.
IoT Devices have different characteristics when compared to other clients such as browsers and mobile apps. Specifically, IoT devices:
Are often embedded systems with no human operator.
Can be deployed in remote locations, where physical access is expensive.
May have limited power and processing resources.
May have intermittent, slow or expensive internet connectivity



## Slide 4: Protocols

Once the data has been collected, it requires a medium for transport. In other words, a communication channel between sensors and the cloud is necessary. IoT Protocols are responsible for transferring data in the online world and this transmission is only possible if two devices are safely connected. IoT standards and protocols involve an invisible language allowing physical objects to communicate with one another.
The choice of network depends on a lot of factors; power consumption, speed of data transfer, range, bandwidth & overall efficiency. Some of the most popular IoT wireless protocols and standards include Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, ZigBee, cellular, etc.
IoT Gateways translate network protocols ensuring seamless communication of all devices within the network. Incoming, raw data from sensors must pass through gateways to reach the cloud.
Moreover, gateways offer security by protecting the system from unauthorized access and malicious attacks. Gateways can also preprocess data from the sensors before sending it to the cloud, in other words minimize large volumes of data to reduce strain on the network and minimize the amount of processing required in the cloud.



## Slide 5: Cloud Services

Once the data has been collected and it has travelled to the cloud, it needs to be processed. 
The cloud is where the “smart stuff” takes place. In an IoT system, the cloud services typically:
Receive telemetry at scale from devices and determine how to store and process that data.
Analyse the telemetry to provide insights, either in real time or after the fact.
Send commands from the cloud to specific devices.
Provision devices and control which devices can connect to the infrastructure.
Control the state of devices and monitor their activities.
Manage the firmware installed on your devices.
All of this is performed for colossal amounts of data in just under milliseconds. Time is critical for IoT, as especially in critical concerns such as health and safety, latency cannot be compromised.
With their immense computing power, storage capabilities, networking options, analytics and other service components, clouds make information effectively available for the consumers.

## Slide 7: Pros & Cons

Pros: IoT devices can provide convenience, safety and health benefits for individuals and society.
IoT devices can enable automation and efficiency in various areas, such as manufacturing, agriculture, transportation and logistics. 
IoT devices provide massive amounts of data that can be used for analysis, optimization and innovation.
Cons: IoT devices can be hacked or compromised by malicious actors to expose sensitive personal information or be used for surveillance or espionage. 
IoT devices can increase the complexity and vulnerability of network infrastructure. 
IoT devices can have negative social and environmental impacts as in reducing human interaction or skills.
