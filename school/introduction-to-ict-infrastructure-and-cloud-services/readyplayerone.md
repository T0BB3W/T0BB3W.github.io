# How does the book describe the future?

In the real/physical world of the book, there is a global energy crisis, fossil fuels are depleted causing economic collapse and widespread poverty. Climate change has caused extreme weather and environmental degradation, leading to mass migration, overcrowding of major cities, leading to the creation of “stacks” which are basically vertical trailer parks made of RV’s and mobile homes stacked on top of each other.

A sort of fascist environment can be sensed, since large corporations have amassed unprecedented power over society, leading to extreme wealth gaps.

The OASIS is a massive multiplayer online simulation that has become the go-to escape from reality. OASIS has its own currency (credits) that is more valuable than real-world money. Virtual planets and properties within the OASIS have real monetary value. People seem to care more about their digital identity than that of their physical selves. Many people work jobs entirely within the OASIS.

# How does the book describe the past?

The novel is deeply nostalgic for the 1980’s. Halliday’s contest forces an entire generation to become experts in 1980’s culture, creating a weird resurrection of this era, within the futuristic OASIS.

# How do people use computers in the book?

OASIS Immersion rings range from basic haptic gloves and visors, all the way to advanced full haptic bodysuits, omnidirectional treadmills and smell generators. These provide ultimate sensory experiences and tactile feedback for the users. High-resolution VR displays with surround sound are also common.

ONI (OASIS Network Interface) is the operating system that powers the OASIS experience.

Neural interfaces are also a thing but not for everyone, given their steep pricing. They connect directly to user’s nervous systems. Mobile access is also available through portable OASIS consoles that allow limited access on the go.

Most users spend most of their waking hours logged into the OASIS metaverse. Users give great thought to their avatar appearance and reputation, so they can participate in the digital economy, working, shopping, and investing within the OASIS.

Education is also arranged inside the OASIS, in the form of virtual schools.

Users form friendships and romantic relationships within the OASIS, and can enjoy entertainment in the form of gaming, watching films and even attending concerts.

# What was the role of the computer, and how is information technology used in the book?

The role of the computer and systems is massive. The OASIS has replaced physical gathering spaces; the virtual economy is more important and stable than real-world economies. The OASIS provides escape from the harsh reality of the physical world.

Technically the simulation is described as a perfectly realistic physics and sensory experience. The OASIS uses data collection to learn about users, and global real-time communication is the standard. Both GSS (Gregarious Simulation Systems) and IOI (Innovative Online Industries) monitor user activities. Authentication-wise the identity verification is crucial in the contest. Massive databases store the entirety of human media and knowledge.

The contest is central for deciding on who will control the future of the OASIS.

# The world described in the book, is that kind of world possible?

Yes and no. We are already seeing rapid development in VR technology in today’s day and age. Basic haptic feedback technology exists and is also improving constantly. Our internet infrastructure continues to expand at exponential rates given the inflation of data and compute. Digital economies, such as cryptocurrencies, NFT’s, etc. Already exist and are growing in market share at a steady pace. Also, neural implants have seen large leaps in the last years, however they are being explored as medical tools for now, not as providing sensory experiences for leisure.

The least plausible elements would be that the level of sensory detail described would require more processing power than we are on track to develop by 2045. Energy requirements are also a concern, since we barely can allocate enough energy as is, to power enormous data centres and the like. Latency/network issues are also barely addressed in the book; these are crucial for such a system to work. The size and complexity of the OASIS would require compute beyond what’s likely available by 2045.

Socially, the field is already prepared for such a change, given escapism through technology and media is already a worrying trend. The increasing corporate power and control over the economy, and digital spaces is also observable today, being fuelled by the AI race. Social media is also showing us that many people, value digital identities more than any other metric.

# What kind of user interface did they use?

Full sensory immersive technology with sophisticated haptic, visual and audio feedback, as described earlier.

# Your notes and observations of the book.

My personal observations of the book are that it’s completely dystopian. I have read many similar books as well, such as Brave New World, 1984, and I think everyone should. These books were merely fiction some tens of years ago, but they serve as a warning of what might come. There really is no limit to the evil of certain individuals and corporations in the world, and learning to identify these complications early on, are crucial as not to fall into the claws of fascism and technocracy. However, in the end of the book, Wade wins and puts new measures in place, which teaches a lesson that there is always hope.

# Reflecting on the book and our world, what differences are noticed in the usage of computers, user interfaces, services and applications?

Reflecting on Ready Player One and our world, technological differences come to light in how we engage with digital spaces. While our digital landscape requires juggling several devices, platforms and interfaces with limited sensory feedback and clear boundaries between being online or offline, the OASIS represents a complete, fully immersive alternative reality system, where this boundary has nearly dissolved.
