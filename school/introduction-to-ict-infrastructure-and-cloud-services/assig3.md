Resistor

                         Resistors are part of almost all electronics, in computer components,
                         GPU’s, Processors, RAM, you name it. Physically resistors are just
                         cylindrical or rectangular components with two wires coming out of each
                         end.

                         Resistors do exactly what the name tells, it resists the flow of electricity,
                         regulating how much electricity can pass through, and the resistance is
                         measured in Ohms (Ω). Resistors have distinct features, usually different
                         coloured lines to tell the resistance.

                         Sometimes resistors or transistors in computer components can break or
                         come loose due to various reasons, you can even fix your components if
                         you can pinpoint the point of failure. Fixing them will require skill and
                         experience using volt meters, soldering irons and so on. There are even
                         quick fixes that work when the legs of components have failed due to bad
                         solder joints or minor manufacturing defects, for example Oven Reflowing
                         when you just bake the entire circuit board affected in the oven to fix some
                         if not all of the forementioned issues.
